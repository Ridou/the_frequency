# Christian Rand (Chris)

**Name Origin:** Anagram of Richard Santin

## Basic Info
- **Age:** Late 20s / early 30s
- **Occupation:** Tech support
- **Role:** The Thirteenth - Completes the circle of twelve

## Personality
- Regular guy, relatable
- Loves baseball (Dodgers fan)
- Philosophical, thinks deeply
- Natural instinct to help/amplify rather than control
- Questions things but willing to commit once convinced

## Abilities
- **Natural Talent:** Extraordinary speed of learning
- **Awakening Trigger:** Game 7 of World Series (Miguel Rojas home run)
- **First Power:** Amplification (respects free will)
- **Core Strength:** Instinctively does things "the right way" without training

## Skills Learned (So Far)
1. **Amplification** - Enhancing others' natural abilities
2. **Folding** - Teleportation through space (23 successful folds in first session)
3. **Cloaking/Dissolving** - Hiding presence in the frequency
4. **Sensing** - Detecting other frequency users, threat assessment

## Character Arc
- Day 1: Awakening during baseball game
- Day 2: Meets Ren, learns about the Society
- Day 3: Meets The Apostles, begins training
- Day 4: Masters folding (unprecedented speed)
- Current: Learning to filter perception, encountering Controllers

## Relationships
- **Ren:** Best friend for 3 years, mentor, guide (was watching over him)
- **The Twelve:** Teachers, protectors, believers in his potential
- **The Controllers (Vera, The Serpent):** Want to recruit or eliminate him

## Internal Conflicts
- Speed of advancement (too fast? burning out?)
- Karmic cost of using abilities
- Prophecy burden - is he really "the one"?
- Choice between Controllers and Apostles
- Maintaining humanity while developing superhuman abilities

## Key Moments
1. First amplification - Miguel Rojas homer
2. Second amplification - Will Smith homer
3. First rejection - Yamamoto pushing back ("I don't need it")
4. First fold - teleporting across Griffith Park
5. First Controller contact - Vera's approach

## Development Notes
- Already demonstrates wisdom (respecting Yamamoto's mastery)
- Natural filter for threat assessment
- Accepts karmic cost rather than seeking shortcuts
- Chooses to hear all sides before judging
- Growing confidence without arrogance
